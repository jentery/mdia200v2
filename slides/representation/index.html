The Logic of Representation

Representation in media is a form of social control.

Media demonstrate power through exclusion.

Media reflect cultural power through stereotypes.

Marginalized audiences internalize stereotypes.

Media use genre to naturalize stereotypes.

Marginalized groups use appropriation,
counter-types, and deconstruction to
transcode stereotypes.

Here, I'm drawing from the work of Stuart Hall.

Personal + Social in Media

The interplay of personal and social identities means our identifications with media --- our experiences of feeling seen --- are partial or contradictory.

I can, for instance, identify with a fantasy character without assuming I am that character.

The act of transcoding a stereotype engages
such contradictions, while vulgar expressions of
power (such as stereotypes) aim to "flatten"
complexity for social control.

Recalling Ownership Effects

These questions matter in a moment of "media
convergence," where audiences are kited across
formats and platforms in search of more and
more entertainment.

A book becomes a video game becomes a comic
becomes a tv show, with hundreds of reaction vids
and streams along the way.

Media empires usually own the IP for these
stories, and they tend to be risk-averse, favouring what's predictable or expected in the interests of
their ROI (return on investment).

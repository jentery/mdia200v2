The Content Industry

When content is treated as filler

Format and media don't matter (just "content")

Goal is to increase time on device

"Time on device" is a term from casino and machine gambling design.
Check out Natasha Dow Schüll's Addiction by Design to learn more about it.

Some Implications

Puts content creators in precarious positions
(a "gig economy" with low, task-based wages).

People are encouraged to constantly circulate
content to amplify their brand (ancillary content
about social status accrues "content capital").

See Amazon's "Mechanical Turk" platform for an example of gig economics. They describe it as
"a global, on-demand, 24x7 workforce." Most of their "Human Intelligence Tasks" (HITs) pay workers a
few cents to a few dollars per task. Content creation is among those tasks, and the HIT pay for it tends
to be higher than average on the platform.

Another Implication

The already vulnerable become even more
vulnerable online:

"Some young people are more vulnerable to
cybervictimization, including Indigenous youth,
sexually diverse and non-binary youth, youth
with a disability, and girls and women"
(Hango 2023 for Public Safety Canada).

One More Implication

It's difficult to moderate inaccurate, harmful,
exploitative, and hateful content.

And the low-wage job of moderation is taxing.

See next slide (an excerpt of an interview with
Sarah Roberts) for more about content moderation.

Roberts wrote the influential book, Behind the Screen: Content Moderation in the Shadows of Social Media.

Prompt: Tracing the Instagram Egg

The Instagram Egg is part of the so-called
"content industry," where content circulates to
fill people's feeds and increase their time on device.

Let's trace that circulation and test that assertion.

Prompt cont.: Tracing the Instagram Egg

Identify three places where the Instagram Egg
appeared online and sparked
meaningful engagement.

Then use no more than 250 words to tell me about these appearances and what was
meaningful about their engagements.

You're welcome to use point form and don't forget
to cite all three of the Egg's appearances,
including their URLs.

ephemeral effects. Be sure to address that challenge.

effect on your mood or feelings. Your three
interpretations of it (direct, minimal, third-person)
will contradict each other. And that's ok.

hardly ever supported by science.

Data, Access, and Trust

Affording people access to your data is often
associated with transparent + trustworthy
practices as well as credible, research-based
arguments for those practices.

A lack of data or a refusal to share data may
thus spark suspicion within communities.

However, data alone does not solve problems,
and collecting (more) data won't necessarily
address your questions or problems.

Prompt 3

1) describe a missing dataset you wish existed,
2) explain why it should exist,
3) identify which community or social group would benefit most from access to it,
4) state how you would benefit from access to it, and
5) communicate why you believe or know
the dataset doesn't exist.

Ọnụọha on Why
Data's Missing

"Those who have the resources to collect data lack
the incentive to (corollary: often those who have
access to a dataset are the same ones who have
the ability to remove, hide, or obscure it)."

"The data to be collected resist simple quantification
(corollary: we prioritize collecting things that fit our modes of collection)."

"The act of collection involves more work than the
benefit the presence of the data is perceived to give."

"There are advantages to nonexistence."
